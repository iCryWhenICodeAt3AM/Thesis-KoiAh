Model Training Dataset

Our dataset features a comprehensive split between koi and non-koi categories, offering a rich variety of images and annotations to capture the unique characteristics of each group.

Koi Varieties

Our koi dataset features several distinct varieties:

Kohaku with white and red patterns

Sanke with red and black markings

Showa with black bodies and red or white spots

Hikarimono known for its metallic sheen, Asagi with light blue bodies and red undersides

Bekko with black markings on white, red, or yellow bodies

Non-koi Objects

Our non-koi dataset includes a variety of objects:

Goldfish with diverse colors and patterns

People captured in various settings

Lilies featuring vibrant and detailed floral images

Rocks showcasing different textures and formations

For our hypothesis one experiment, we utilized a balanced dataset with:

302 images of koi varieties

This dataset was used to evaluate and compare the performance of our models across both categories.

Model Training Table

The model used for this hypothesis is trained using a koi fish and non-koi objects dataset.

For the koi fish, 6 varieties of koi fish had been used, which include:

Kohaku, Sanke, Showa, Hikarimono, Asagi, and Bekko.

For the non-koi objects, 4 objects had been used, which include:

Goldfish, People, Lilies, and Rocks.

All of which have 500 images each, further subdivided into solo, grouped, and varied.


Categories description are as follows:

Solo: one class and one instance of the class.

Grouped: one class and multiple instances of the class.

Varied: multiple classes.

Classes can be defined as a group of items identified during model training. E.g. Kohaku, Sanke, Showa, etc.

Hypothesis: One Table

For hypothesis one experimentation, the dataset used was categorized into Koi and Non-koi images. The dataset was further subdivided into Solo, Grouped, and Varied categories. Below is the breakdown of the images used:

Solo: This category includes images with one class and one instance of that class. The dataset contains 218 images of Koi and 143 images of Non-koi objects.

Grouped: This category includes images with one class but multiple instances of that class. There are 62 images of Koi and 157 images of Non-koi objects in this category.

Varied: This category includes images with multiple classes. The dataset has 22 images of Koi and 2 images of Non-koi objects.

Total: Overall, there are 302 images of Koi and 302 images of Non-koi objects used for testing.

Hypothesis: Two Table

For hypothesis to experimentation, the dataset used was categorized into different size groups: Small, Medium, and Large. The dataset was further subdivided into Solo, Grouped, and Varied categories. Below is the breakdown of the images used:

Solo: This category includes images with one class and one instance of that class. The dataset contains 106 images of Small, 101 images of Medium, and 142 images of Large sizes.

Grouped: This category includes images with one class but multiple instances of that class. There are 39 images of Small, 0 images of Medium, and 9 images of Large sizes in this category.

Varied: This category includes images with multiple classes. The dataset has 10 images of Small, 54 images of Medium, and 4 images of Large sizes.

Total: Overall, there are 155 images each of Small, Medium, and Large sizes used for testing.
